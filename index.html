Grand Challenges Scholars Program

Removing Nuclear Waste from Earth

Team Members: Adam Cabezas, Mudit Garg, Jaiden Juarez, Ethan Teo

Our Grand Challenges Scholars Program project addresses one of humanity's most pressing environmental challenges: the safe disposal of nuclear waste. We developed an innovative solution to transport nuclear waste to the Sun using advanced rocket propulsion technology.

Key Technologies:

  • Super-Rotational Kinetic Slingshot - A rocket launcher using centrifugal force to reach escape velocity without fuel
  • Small Gimbal Engine - A chemical engine for trajectory course correction past the Karman Line
  • Gravity Assist - Leveraging gravitational fields of celestial bodies for propulsion
  • Advanced Trajectory Computers - Physics-based prediction and path calculation systems

Impact: This project explores cutting-edge solutions to nuclear waste management while contributing to environmental sustainability and space exploration technologies. Our research demonstrates the intersection of engineering innovation and global challenge-solving.
